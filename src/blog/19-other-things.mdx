I watched Project Hail Mary this week. Usually I never go to see movies. I enjoy it, I am not saying
that I don't. I just don't ever think about doing it. If there is something that I want to go and watch
I just do it alone most of the time. I don't event think about inviting anyone. Project Hail Mary is
one of the few movies I had wanted to watch and one I wanted to go to with people and after a few failed
attempts, DD and JFK and I were able to agree to a plan. We booked an extra ticket for Champion 
without telling her. She believed we were joking (she had already seen it) until the last day when
we called her to tell her she has to come.

It's a good movie. I like it because it's a friendship movie disguised as a space movie. There is
not a lot of _space stuff_  in it. It's also unrealistic like all space movies. Usually I am the
nerd who complains about these silly things but this time I just let the movie roll. I have started
expecting sad endings from movies and the movie played me like a fool, it only has a sad beginning.
Overall 10/10. Would watch again.

Saturday I woke up feeling a little weak. I spent most of my day on the bed without scrolling my phone.
I didn't feel like doing much that day nor did I have any energy in me for anything. I spent most of
my afternoon thinking about how I have had a vinyl record for a long time and I have not listened to
it yet. I have wanted to own a turntable for a very long time and especially after I was given the
this record. By 5pm the thought of listening the album had become
an irresistible urge and with it came the energy to get out of my bed. I decided I'll go and at least
look at what I want to buy and how much it would cost and so I left for the nearest shop that would help
me with that. When I reached and informed the guy running the store that I want to buy a turntable
and I don't know anything about it, he asked me to sit in a room and played a record.
After 5 seconds he said don't think about it and just buy "this". I had seen it before. During my research
other had said the same thing, that if I am starting out and don't plan on upgrading a lot and don't
want to spend a lot then "this", the turntable the guy showed me, is the one. I told him I'll think about
it and he tells me "It's not that deep bro". First of all that is my line, secondly not something you
should say to an impulsive person. After that I did not think about it at all. I just bought it.
I am happy with the decision though. Usually that does not happen when I buy things impulsively. So I
have another very expensive hobby now. I have a very easy answer to the question "what do you want for
your birthday?" now.
