Everything that I see going on with the world feels overbearing. I have the thought that the quality 
of this bizarre manifestation that we call life has been going down hill for as long as I remember. 
A day doesn't go by without reminiscing about better days. I walk down through my memory lane remembering 
those days. Some things in recent years, like how I used to romanticize being in Bangalore and that 
I enjoyed the freedom it brought me. Presently, it's just a feeling I remember having. All I have
now is the frustration that comes from how overbearing the city has become.
Other times it's something from further back in my past, like spending my summers playing cricket. 
The feeling of sweat beads rolling down my forehead after playing all morning to my heart's content is
something I have missed since 10th grade. A hop, skip, and a jump away from the field was home where,
after playing, I ate watermelon for hours, spitting the seeds from my balcony and trying to launch
each one farther than the last. These are luxuries of a simpler life. I know it is better.
I just don't know how to have it.

Maybe I feel life is worse because that's all I get to know. I spend my waking hours being connected
to the internet like everyone else. If I have nothing to do, I always find myself scrolling
through Reddit or Instagram and I witness things that I can only call a tragedy. People dying due
to negligence by the authorities in my country. People dying because of an unnecessary war. Just the
terrible state of everything. In the comments I see discord of people, deranged and out of touch with reality.
It surprises me that people will always find something to disagree about and argue over in the most
vile manner. You have to be a special kind of stupid to justify people dying, be it because of war
or because a bridge fell in Mumbai. I hate these keyboard warriors even if they share my sentiments.
Am I supposed to feel good knowing that half the world is suffering while people on the internet
argue whether the suffering is real?

I could be wrong. I could train my feed to show me better things and that's all my subconscious will
know and I won't feel hopeless all the time. Some would call that being ignorant. I would. It does
not matter, though. I will trade having a minute of mental peace for not being ignorant.
I feel it would hardly make a difference. I am insignificant in this swarm of polar masses.

Though I can't be ignorant of the things I see in real life. I see the destitute and neglected state
that we have accepted. I don't even know why I complain. I am much better off than most people. I just
feel frustrated seeing the sad conditions the people of my here have to deal with.

Sometimes I taste dust while riding my bike. Bangalore has been dust-laden with all the never ending 
construction that has been going on. Dust tastes like dust. I don't know how to explain it, it's the
earthiest taste you could probably experience. When I was a little kid my friends and I used to play
football on a small, grassless patch near my home. We stirred up a plume of dust every time we played
and that's where I know the taste from. Out of breath, I would sit down, gasping, and swallow the dust
we had kicked up. I liked the taste then. I used to think of it as a reward, something you earned only
after running hard enough, playing long enough. To taste it was proof that I had given everything.
My world was small then. I did not know that there were green, clean fields where children played
without raising clouds of earth. That patch of bare ground was all I knew, and I was happy in it.

In contrast, when I taste dust now, I do not enjoy it. It is not a reward. It is simply there, given to me,
and I must accept it. The former was a sign of joy, the latter a sign of frustration.

I find this declining quality in food as well. Everyone enjoys a good meal. It's funny to me when
someone says "I love eating. I'm a foodie." Yeah no shit, that's like the whole point of being
alive. I am more shocked when someone I know says that they are not very keen on trying new things.
Either they are depressed or way past material desire, and in both cases I have nothing good to say to them.
When I first came to Bangalore, I was happy because now all of a sudden I had all these options of food that I never had before.
I ate everything I could for the last two years and I had fun. More recently, that excitement has
died. I just don't have an appetite for it anymore. A few times in the last month I dumped everything
I had ordered. Usually I'll eat anything, but those really were just bad. I don't know if it is me 
or if the quality of food has actually become worse. I honestly feel it's the food. At times I think
about the person who made my food and I imagine an overworked, underpaid person who has been following
the same recipe for many years. Now his boss has started to bring low-quality ingredients and asks to
put less of everything. He does not enjoy cooking anymore and wants to leave his job and go back home.
I sympathize with that person even if the food in front of me is diabolical. Maybe they are just bad
cooks who do not care, and I am trying to justify their incompetence.

I do have to eat something, so I now cook all my meals myself. It's much better in every sense.
It takes up my whole day, though. I watched The Lunchbox recently and there is a scene where Shaikh,
played by Nawazuddin Siddiqui, is chopping vegetables on his office files while commuting on the train
to make good use of his time. It highlights a real problem in a funny way, people in India don't have
time for themselves. Soon I'll be like Shaikh, chopping vegetables on the laptop my boss went to so
much effort to get me.

Some of you must be thinking "Job? Boss? Laptop? Has Anirudh traded his unshackled life for days of mediocrity?" 
The answer is yes, but it's not mediocre. I have been enjoying it.
I get to work on one thing all day without being interrupted every 5 minutes. I can actually try to
look at what I have been doing and think of making it better. Anyway, in software you can only try
to make things better, everything is always a mess. Regardless, I like having the luxury and, the people
are nice. Sometimes I worry I am unqualified for the job, but I try to remember the words of my Romate, 
either there was no one better, at which point it does not matter, or they actually trust me to get
the job done and I should trust myself too.

I always worry about what I know and what I don't and how I can know more. I am trying not to do that
here. I look at my work and I ask myself, "Is this good? Would I enjoy using this?" If the answer is
no, then I keep working on it. It helps me get started with my work rather than worrying about what
the absolute best way to do it is. The best way does not exist, all software will always be terrible.

The job takes up a lot of time of my day but it's fine; I knew that before I joined. I've been making
sure to take out some time to work out every day. I met with _H_ sometime in March an old friend
from a long time ago. While catching up he talked to me about working out and suggested I try that
instead of doing nothing. I was inspired. He was also right. I would like to be in better shape.
Right now I don't feel healthy. I feel as if it's my job to drag this creaky skeleton around at the
moment. The thought of being at my absolute physical peak is very exciting. I have had the fun of not
doing anything for a few years now. I think I'll enjoy spending my time now toward a goal. So that's
all I do these days I work so that I can cook myself a meal, I cook so that I can work out.

Some day in the future I'd like to stop doing all this and have a much slower life. For now this works

> "Perhaps one day, tired of circling the world, I'll return to Argentina and settle in the Andes lakes,
if not indefinitely then at least for a pause while I shift from one understanding of the world to another" 

I have taken a pause not from traveling per se, but from myself.
