Everything that I see going on with the world feels overbearing. I have the thought that the quality 
of this bizarre manifestation that we call life has been going down hill for as long as I remember. 
A day doesn't go by without reminiscing of better days. I walk down through my memory lane remembering 
those days. Somethings in recent years, like how I used to romanticize being in Bangalore and that 
I enjoyed the freedom it brought me. Presently, It's just a feeling I remember having. All I have
now is the frustration that comes from how overbearing the city has become.
Other times it's something from well in my past like spending my summers playing cricket. 
The feeling of sweat beads rolling down my forehead after playing all morning to my wills content is
something I have missed ever since my 10th grade. A hop, skip, and a jump away from the field was
home where, after playing I ate watermelons for hours, spitting the seeds from my balcony trying to
get each one further than the last one. These are luxuries of a simpler life. I know it is better.
I just don't know how to have it.

Maybe I feel life is worse because that's all I get to know. I spend my waking hours being connected
to the internet like everyone else. If I have nothing to do, I always find myself scrolling
through Reddit or Instagram and I witness things that I can only call a tragedy. People dying due
to negligence of the authorities of my country. People dying because of an unnecessary war. Just the
terrible state of everything. In the comments I see discord of people. Deranged and out of touch with reality.
It surprises that people will always find something to disagree and argue about in the most vile manner.
You have to be a special kind of stupid to justify people dying. Be it because of a war or because a
bridge fell in Mumabi. I hate these keyboard warriors even if they share my sentiments. I feel disgusted,
and I am not exaggerating, to know I walk on the same planet as these people. 
Am I supposed feel good knowing the half of the world is suffering and there are people on internet
arguing if the suffering is real.

I could be wrong. I could train my feed to show me better things and that's all my subconscious will
know and I won't feel hopeless all the time. Some would call that being ignorant. I would. It does not
matter though. I will trade having a minute of mental peace for not being ignorant. I feel it would 
hardly make a difference. I am insignificant in this swarm of polar masses.

Though I can't be ignorant to the things I see in real life. I see the destitute and neglected state
that we have accepted. I don't even know why I complain, I much better off than most people. I just
feel frustrated seeing the sad conditions the people of my naation have to deal with.

Sometimes I taste dust while riding my bike. Bangalore has been dust laden with all the never ending 
construction that has been going on.
Dust tastes like dust. I don't know how to explain it. It's the earthiest earthy taste you could
probably experience. When I was a little kid my friends and I used to play football on a small,
grass-less patch near my home. We stirred up a plume of dust every time we played and that's where
I know the taste from. Out of breath, I would sit down, gasping, and swallow with it the dust we had
kicked up. I liked the taste then. I used to think of it as a reward, something you earned only after
running hard enough, playing long enough. To taste it was proof that I had given everything.
My world was small then. I did not know that there were green, clean fields where children played
without raising clouds of earth. That patch of bare ground was all I knew, and I was happy in it.
Contrary, When I taste dust now, I do not enjoy it. it is not a reward. it is simply there, given to
me, and I must accept it. The former is a sign of and joy. the latter of frustration. 

I find this declining quality in food as well. Everyone enjoys a good meal. It's funny to me when
someone says "I love eating. I am foodie". Yeah no shit idiot that's like the whole point of being
alive. I am more shocked when someone I know says that they are not very keen on trying new things.
Either they are depressed or way past material desire and in both cases I have nothing good to say to them.
When I first came to Bangalore, I was happy because now all of a sudden I had all these options of food
that I never had before. I ate everything I could for last two years and I had fun. More recently,
that excitement has died. I just don't have appetite for it anymore. A few times in the last month,
I dumped everything I had ordered. Usually I'll anything but those really were just bad. I don't know
if it is me or the quality of food has actually become worse. I honestly feel it's the food. At times
I think about the person who made my food and I Imagine an overworked, underpaid guy who has been
following the same recipe for many year. Now his boss has started to bring low quality ingredients
and asks to put less of everything. He does not enjoy cooking anymore and want's to leave his job
and go back home. I sympathize with that person even if the food in front of my is diabolical.
Maybe they are just a bad cooks who do not give a shit and I am trying to justify their incompetence.

I do have to eat something so I just now cook all meals myself. It's much better in every sense. It
takes all my day though.  
I watched the lunchbox recently and there is a scene where Shaikh, played by Nawazuddin Siddiqui,
is chopping vegetables on his office files while commuting in the train to make good use of his time.
It highlights a real problem in a funny way. People in India don't have time for themselves. Soon I'll
be like Shaikh, chopping vegetables on my laptop that my boss spent too much effort getting me. 

Some of you all must be thinking "Job? Boss? Laptop? Has Anirudh traded in his as an unshackled man
for days of mediocrity?" The answer is yes but it's not mediocre. I have been enjoying it.
I get to work on one thing all day without being interrupted every 5 minutes. I can actually try to
look at what I have been doing and think of making it better. Anyway in software you can only try to
make things better, everything is always a mess. Regardless, I like having the luxury and the people
are nice. Sometimes I worry I am unqualified for the job but I try to remember the words of Roomate.
Either there was no one better, at which point it does not matter or they actually trust me to get
the job done and I should too.

I always worry about what I know and what I don't and how I can know more. I am trying not to do
that here. I look at my work and I ask myself if this is good? Would I enjoy using this? If the answer
is no then I keep working on it. It helps me get started with my work rather than worrying about what
is the absolute best way to do it. The best way does not exist, all software will always be terrible.

The job takes up a lot of time of my day but it's fine, I knew that before I joined. I've been making 
sure to take out some to work out every day. I met with _H_ sometime in March. An excellent friend 
from a long time ago. While catching up he talked to me about working out. How I should try that 
instead of not doing anything. I was inspired. He was also right. I would like to be in better shape. 
Right now I don't feel healthy. I feel as if it's job to drag this creaky skeleton around at the moment.
The thought of being at my absolute physical peak is very exciting. I have had the fun of not doing
anything for a few years now. I think I'll enjoy spending my time now towards a goal. So that's all
I do these days. I work so that I can cook myself a meal, I cook so that I can workout.
Amidst being the busiest I have been in all my life, I worry about the nature things and their
declining quality. I just wish I had one more hour in my day. I wouldn't do much I think I'd just
like to sleep longer or eat fruits leisurely, just to enjoy the taste and not to keep myself healthy.

During a dinner, my boss said he would like to go back to Lucknow some day during the mango season
only to eat mangoes. "Balti bhar ke aam" was the term he used. Funny enough two days later another friend
said the same thing. It has been on my mind ever since. It's a much simpler and better life. I want
it back. I just don't know how to have it.

I have been asked by a few people when would I travel again and where I would like to go. Honestly,
I have not thought about it. Right now I am enjoying what I have been given and I'd like to do it for
as long as I can. I had been reading The Motorcycle diaries and I think this line from the book sums
it well for how I feel.

> "Perhaps one day, tired of circling the world, I'll return to Argentina and settle in the Andes lakes,
if not indefinitely then at least for a pause while I shift from one understanding of the world to another" 

I have taken a pause, not from traveling per se. Just from myself.
