I came across the line "I yearn for precedented times." in the Youtube comment section of a MoistCr1TiKaL
video about residents in USA having to look for a new energy supplier as their existing one will start
focusing on AI data center. I laughed harder than I have in a really long time once I read that. I can't
explain how much I found it funny, especially considering the context of the comment is something really
concerning. I don't even know what made me laugh so hard. I think it's just the fact that I was either
expecting bot comments or one of the copious comments that start with "bro thinks". Sadly it only had
3 likes which leads me to the conclusion that people on the internet have no sense of humour. At the
same time it is very relatable as well. I think a lot of people are yearning for precedented times.
The unprecedented current affairs are too much for anyone, with or without AI.

I spent a couple of weeks at home away from Bangalore. It was a time of mourning but I think I don't
register these things that well. The entire time I was home I was worried about getting my work done.
While I was working I was worried about spending time with my family. I am like that cow meme that
thinks about eating grass while chilling and thinks about chilling while eating grass. 

![An image of a cow that is thinking about eating grass while resting and below it there is an image of the same cow thinking about resting while eating grass](https://theblog-static.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/week21/week21_cow_meme.webp "Me IRL")

One day
while working I thought about chilling so much that I slept the entire afternoon thinking that I'll
finish the rest of the work after getting up but when I woke up my mother had decided that I need
to drive her and my sister to the hospital and all I could think about was working. I carried my
laptop to the hospital so that I could work there not have the guilt of sleeping. In Bangalore that
would be considered normal. Back home seeing a laptop in the wild is already insane enough let alone
someone working on it.

I enjoyed my free time though, I spent a good amount of time with one of my cousin. I used to spend
a lot of time with him when I was a little kid but as we grew older I guess we just had less time
to do what kids do. It was fun learning what he is upto these days and talking about music with
him. Street food is his favourite thing to have so one day he asked me to take him somewhere for momos.
I have not been to my home home properly since covid ended and my college started so I took him to the
place I used to go to with my friends when I was in school. Not much had changed about it
except that it was much bigger that I remembered it.

![Entrance to a small shop named Kathmandu that sells momos](https://theblog-static.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/week21/week21_kathmandu.jpeg "This is the place")

One of the last day of my stay, my sister, three of my cousins and I went out to just spend time away from
all the grown ups. We meet very infrequently and when we do it's usually at times like these so it's
more of doing chores and less of just hanging out.  
When I go back I try to take sometime out and meet my friends there. In the last few year
that small slice of time I manage to take out shrunken even more. This time I only managed to meet Observer and Chel.
I can't remember if I have ever mentioned Observer before and if I have then what did I name him.
We basically just walked in circles for while while briefly catching up. Good to see him. I wish
I had more time to do stupid things like we used to rather than just saying hello for 30 minutes.

Meeting Chel was very unexpected because I never expect to meet her. The last time I saw her was
February of 2020 and after that she left India and never came back (as far as I am concerned). Before
that we had spent many a days only god know doing what, I honestly can't put my finger on what we used to do
I only remember it was fun<sup><a href="#notes">[1]</a></sup>. The only possible way to meet her was at the airport
after she landed and before her family decided it's go time so like 30 minutes.

### Music Listening and Miscellenous Activties
A spent a fortune on getting more vinyls so that spending a fortune on the turntable actually makes
sense. I know right, ridiculous. I remember when people used to compare buying an iphone to selling
kidneys and now no one cares. iphone has become the normal now, at least the broader circle that I am
a part of. Owning this antiquated device is what many will consider to be kidney selling level now.
I honestly get it, that's how I felt buying more records. Phones do a lot more, this on the other hand
plays music form a disc that is too fragile to hold it's own weight.

It really is a novelty. The more
I think about it the less sense it makes. The more I listen to it the more money I want to throw at it.
I enjoy it so far. It sounds great, this is best quality sound I have listened to. It makes listening
to music slightly more fun for me because when I use spotify I always shuffle and go from Skrillex
doing a bass drop to Hozier talking about some church. I think about what I want to listen and skip
songs that don't fall into that category. Listening to a record is simple, I pick one and I have to
sit through it, all songs nicely follow a theme. When a side of the record finishes I have to get up
and flip it to continue which makes it much more involved. And the records sometimes come with fun
things like lyrics sheet or posters or just pictures of the recording sessions. It actually feels
like collecting music (yeah I know sum talk about collecting music from a guy who preaches piracy).

![](https://theblog-static.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/week21/week21_record.jpeg "Opening a record and finding there is more than just vinyl is fun")

It's also really annoying at times. I have to get up to play music. The slightest amount of dust completely
ruins the sound so I have to store all of the records very carefully and dust them before playing.
Vinyls are too easy to scratch and bend so I have to be very delicate with them. I can't carry it anywhere and
If I want to listen to more music on the turntable I have to think about selling kidneys again.

One day last week I planned to spend a little longer a work to finish off a few things. While working
all I could think about was what I should have for dinner and kept craving [Kuraku](https://maps.app.goo.gl/YHAiPdKCMsKrVLtR6) the entire time.
I did not make sense because it's too far and why would I go that far to have dinner. I asked _T_ if
she would like to join me, I like taking people to my favourite places and thought if she says yes then
I can tell myself that it's fine to go that far. It was fun, I had a great mango bear and all of my
favourite things there. She kept saying the food is bomb I think means 10/10 but I could be wrong she might
have meant it's maybe a 6 or a 7.
![](https://theblog-static.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/week21/week21_kuraku.jpeg "What we ate")

Another day this week I was just stuck at work late because I could not get a cab back home. After
waiting for an hour I decided to go to DD's place and just hangout with him. Ever since my new job
I see him a little less so I didn't mind catching up. It was not long before AK-47 and JFK showed which
was even better. Nostalgia from previous year had not hit harder before (still reminiscing about better days).
It had been a while since the four of us had been there together so it was fun. We talked about
planting a chip inside humans so that it can tell what exactly is wrong with the body without ever
having to visit the doctor. Good times.
