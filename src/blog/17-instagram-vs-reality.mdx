I have spent the last 15 minutes watching a frame fall off my wall as the adhesive slowly lost all of
its chemical properties that was making the frame stick. Most would have fixed it instead.
I can't really tell why I did that, I had been noticing it come off for a few weeks so I had enough
time to fix it but I wanted to see how it falls. There is a person (dawg) in me that just wants to do things
that don't make any sense. It is often hard to say no to that person and so I agreed (pitched the idea) to go on a trip with
my friend _T_ and two of her friends to Chikmagalur and come back to Bangalore on the same day. 

It sounds like a bad idea because it is one. All things considered, no one should be doing that. I
honestly thought the idea will be met with great opposition and disagreement but my friends agreed. Maybe
they got that dawg in them too. So on Saturday, after a rocky start due to logistic challenges<sup><a href="#references">[1]</a></sup> 
and just 2.5 hours later than planned, we started our trip that was already going downhill even before we reached the mountains.
But hey this is all what we signed up for right? Things that don't make sense.  


![](https://theblog-static.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/week17/IMG_8763.jpeg "How I imagined the distance in my head")

I sometimes feel I need to yap when I am with a group of people who I don't know that well to keep
the conversation going. I don't like when a group is silent but I took the back seat
this time and listened to _girl talk_. I have never been on a trip where I am the only guy before
so the conversations were really a change of pace for me. I asked _S_ how she has so many hobbies. She writes,
plays video games, plays guitar, dances, knows like 10 languages and probably does a few more things.
I am always impressed by people who can pick up so many different things. She tried answering my questions but I
don't think questions like that about hobbies have any real answers.  
_M_ asked me questions about me which I responded
with dumb and silly jokes. Usually people either get annoyed or just smile at my dumb replies but she
clapped back which was also a change of pace.

![](https://theblog-static.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/week17/IMG_8731.jpeg "Group picture. Sorta.")

We stopped at a ridge somewhere near our destination to look at the views after taking a wrong turn.
During my previous travels I had learnt that it is okay to stop and enjoy the views if I want. Yes
I'll be late to my destination but I may also not like it after reaching there. On the other hand
what I am seeing right now is beautiful and I will regret not stopping. I also started a game of
"What Am I?" to keep the day from further de-railing. My worries were in vain though. When we finally
reached it was fun and the views were better. I was hyped. I set out to do something dumb and I had
achieved it. I climbed the edge of a hill to mark my victory and also because it's a skill I am
confident I am fairly good at. Though we were tired and it was getting late, we decided to do last stretch of the hike which was some 300 odd steps to the
peak. We had gone so far it only made sense. I ran through it because why not? On the walk back the sun had started to set and it justified
coming all the way. Some how every time I see the sun set I think that it has been ages since the last time I last saw one.

Travelling for me has changed a lot. It is not so much about the journey or the destination anymore, 
though they are still important. Nor is it about
the people I meet or the food, or chai stalls on the way. As long as I can find something that I enjoy, I am happy.
I asked _T_ and friends if they had fun and they said impromptu plans are the best. I thought about
it while writing this post and I think it does not matter much to me because I know I can find something to enjoy. Be it
asking the group to play a song that I know no one will like.

### Bangalore Bussin'
Few weeks ago Roomate told me that a part of him wants to invite people to ride around Bangalore
in a bus for his birthday (he likes to do things that don't make sense either). Albeit fun I don't
know how good that is for a birthday lol. Few days ago he invited me to travel with him on a bus
for his birthday. He is a fan boy of Bangalore buses, really just look at [this](https://aman.bh/blog/2025/praise-be-to-the-bmtc), and I am an avid public transport enthusiast so
I said yes. I spent Sunday evening (this day) doing just that. It was fun. It's also sad because for many it's the only means of travel and we treat it
as a joy ride. I try not to think too much about though, it's there for us to use. 10/10 would do a 
longer trip around Bangalore soon.
